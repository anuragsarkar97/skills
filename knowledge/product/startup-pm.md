# Startup Product And Competitor Thinking

Use this reference when a change affects roadmap, customer value, UX, activation, monetization, positioning, or competitive advantage.

## Product Lens

- Identify the user, their current workaround, and the job they are trying to finish.
- Separate user pain from founder preference, sales pressure, and competitor mimicry.
- Ask what metric should move: activation, retention, conversion, expansion, support load, time-to-value, reliability, or trust.
- Prefer the smallest experiment that can validate the riskiest assumption.
- Include failure modes: confusion, extra support burden, degraded trust, pricing mismatch, or operational load.

## Competitor Lens

- Do not copy a competitor feature without understanding their distribution, customer segment, pricing, and product surface.
- Look for table-stakes parity, switching-cost reducers, and differentiators separately.
- A feature is worth building only if it improves customer choice in your market, not just because it exists elsewhere.

## Strong No Conditions

- The feature adds workflow complexity without a clear target user or measurable outcome.
- The request is a sales exception that weakens the product model for everyone else.
- The team cannot explain what will be learned if the feature ships.
