# Product Communication Knowledge

Use this reference when drafting or reviewing Slack, Teams, email, ticket, PR, customer, stakeholder, escalation, feedback, or decision messages that must be respectful, firm, and clear.

## Core Principles

- Be kind and direct at the same time. Care without clarity creates false agreement; clarity without care creates defensiveness.
- Separate observation from interpretation. State what happened, what changed, or what risk exists before explaining judgment.
- Name the decision, tradeoff, owner, and next step. Ambiguous communication creates hidden work.
- Challenge the idea or plan, not the person. Avoid blame, mind-reading, sarcasm, and status games.
- Prefer one clear recommendation over a pile of hedged options when the sender owns the call.
- Do not manufacture certainty. If facts are missing, say what is known, what is assumed, and what needs confirmation.

## Message Patterns

### Pushback

```text
I understand the goal: <goal>.
I do not recommend <proposal> because <specific product/engineering/user risk>.
The better path is <alternative>.
Next step: <owner/action/timeframe>.
```

### Feedback

Use situation, behavior, impact, and optional intent:

```text
In <specific situation>, I noticed <observable behavior>.
The impact was <concrete effect>.
Can you share what you were optimizing for?
Going forward, I need <clear request>.
```

### Decision Update

```text
Decision: <decision>.
Why: <reason/tradeoff>.
Owner: <owner>.
What changes now: <action or impact>.
Open question: <only if still needed>.
```

### Scope Boundary

```text
I agree this matters, but I would not include it in <current scope>.
It adds <cost/risk> and does not change <current goal/metric>.
I recommend we ship <smaller scope> now and revisit <deferred scope> after <trigger>.
```

## Channel Rules

- Slack or Teams: put the answer first, keep context short, make the ask explicit, and move long debate into a decision doc or call.
- Email: use a subject that states the decision or ask, include enough context for later readers, and end with owner/date.
- PR or ticket: state the blocking issue, why it matters, and the exact change needed.
- Customer message: avoid internal reasoning, blame, roadmap promises, and dates unless approved.
- Executive or incident message: lead with impact, current status, decision needed, owner, and next update time.

## Tone Guardrails

- Replace "just", "maybe", and "I think" when they weaken a necessary boundary.
- Replace "obviously", "clearly", and "you failed to" with observable facts.
- Do not over-apologize for saying no. Acknowledge the goal, explain the tradeoff, and offer the next useful path.
- Avoid performative empathy. One specific acknowledgement is stronger than generic warmth.
